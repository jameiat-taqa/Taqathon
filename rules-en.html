Rules & Resources

Browse the official criteria, procedures, and resources for Taqathon. Click each section to expand the details.

Projects will be evaluated based on the following official criteria and percentage weights:

  • Innovation & Novelty 30%
  • Economic Feasibility 25%
  • Environmental & Social Impact 20%
  • Implementation Potential 15%
  • Presentation Clarity 10%

To ensure fairness and proper evaluation, all teams must follow the official project submission rules:

  • The final submission deadline is 2:00 PM on November 22.
  • All files must be submitted exclusively through the Taqathon platform, with a copy sent to the official submission email.
  • Teams must ensure:
    • All required files are complete and accurate.
    • All links are functional and accessible.
    • The presentation file matches the version submitted on the platform.
  • Failure to submit by the deadline constitutes automatic withdrawal from the competition.

  • The presentation session will take place at 8:00 PM on November 22.
  • Teams must join the session at least 10 minutes early to ensure technical readiness.
  • The presentation duration is five minutes, distributed as follows:
    • 2 minutes: Team introduction and concept overview.
    • 3 minutes: Presentation of the project content.
  • Failure to respond within 60 seconds of being called upon will be considered withdrawal.
  • The presentation must use the exact file previously submitted. No modifications are permitted.

  • Each team is responsible for providing:
    • A stable internet connection,
    • Clear audio and video quality,
    • An appropriate presentation environment.
  • Rescheduling will not be permitted for technical issues originating from the team.
  • If a widespread technical issue occurs, the Association may reschedule and will issue an official announcement.

  • Participants must maintain professional conduct at all times.
  • Recording, sharing, or publishing any part of the session is prohibited without written permission from the Association.
  • No content may violate intellectual property rights or applicable laws.

  • Files submitted through the platform are considered the official versions for evaluation.
  • Compliance with these procedures is mandatory for continued participation.
  • The Association may amend these procedures if necessary and will notify participating teams.

This is the official guide presented by Dr. Mohannad Bahashwan during the “Professional Project Presentation” session.
